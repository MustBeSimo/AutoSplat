AutoSplat

Turn any photo into a 3D Gaussian Splat

v1.0 · macOS 14+
What it does

AutoSplat uses an on-device AI model to estimate depth from a single 2D image, then generates a full 3D Gaussian Splatting scene you can explore interactively. Drop a photo, click Generate, and orbit your scene in real-time 3D.

Getting started
Download the AI model Open the control panel and click Download Model (~2.67 GB). Or click Select Local Model if you already have a CoreML model on disk.
Drop or open an image Drag a JPG or PNG onto the window, or click Open Image. You can also drop an existing PLY/SPLAT/SPZ file to view it directly.
Generate Click Generate 3DGS. The AI runs entirely on your Mac (Apple Neural Engine + GPU). The 3D viewer opens automatically when done.
3D Viewer

SHARP mode

Camera is fixed at the original capture point. Best for depth perception. Scroll to adjust field of view.

General mode

Free orbit around the 3D model from any angle. Scroll to zoom in/out.

Mouse controls

  • Left-click + drag — Rotate
  • Right-click + drag — Pan
  • Scroll wheel — Zoom / FOV
Control panel
  • Stereo Format — Single View, Side-by-Side, Over/Under, Anaglyph, Parallax Barrier
  • Swap L/R — Swap left and right eye views
  • Depth — Controls stereo eye separation
  • Focus — Controls convergence distance
  • Reset — Resets camera, depth, and focus to defaults
  • Save — Saves the current 3D render as an image
  • Batch Convert — Process multiple images at once

Under the hood

Depth estimation runs via Apple CoreML on the Neural Engine and GPU. The 3D viewer uses Three.js with a WebGL Gaussian Splatting renderer. All processing is local — no data leaves your Mac.
